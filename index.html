ME, ME, ME

Hello, my name is Nick. I’ve been a professional geek for 13 years.

Raised in Bristol

I was lucky enough to be born and raised in Bristol - one of the UK’s great cities.

Happy family

I had a great childhood with brilliant parents. I went to a really good school where I did well academically and had a decent group of friends - some of whom, I still count as my best friends today.

Successful

I carried on doing well - academically and socially - at college and university, and in 2005 I started my career as a professional geek.

Being a techie from Bristol, I didn’t have to look far for decent career opportunities. I’ve had four jobs in my career, and I’ve always moved to the next job as a positive, conscious choice - driven by a motivation to learn new skills and (more recently) to do some good in the world.

I like to think I have a good work ethic, I always want to go a great job, and I always want to make professional progress.

Married

Matt Haig, Reasons to stay alive

You remember I mentioned earlier that I’d once managed to trick someone into being my wife?

Her name is Emma, and we’re still together and going strong - so naturally, the first conversation I had about my stress level was with her.

This was a difficult thing to do. Hearing the words come out of my mouth made me upset. I found it hard to look at her while I was speaking. I didn’t know why it was so hard, but it was.

The second person I spoke to about it was my boss. The conversation was equally as tough.

I have a great relationship with Emma, and a great (but very different) relationship with my boss (both of them). This topic wasn’t difficult for me because I was worried what the other person would think - it was difficult for me because I wasn’t used to talking about what can be an awkward or even taboo issue: MENTAL HEALTH.
